Effective Writing – Simplified: How to Evaluate Your Own LinkedIn Posts

Published: February 6, 2026
Category: Content Strategy

Use this job aid to help you create or evaluate LinkedIn™ posts that reflect your desired writing style, tone, and strategy. This framework will help you craft posts that are clear, impactful, and aligned with your personal brand.

Opening

Line 1: Hook

Make it broad and attention-grabbing. This is your chance to stop the scroll.

Line 2:

Leave it intentionally blank. White space creates visual breathing room and draws the eye to what comes next.

Line 3: Re-hook

  • Keep it under 46 characters
  • Spark curiosity
  • Your opening should summarize the message clearly

Body

Tell a story or deliver a clear lesson. Your body content should be:

  • Punchy: Use scannable sentences that get to the point
  • Spaced: Use white space for mobile readability
  • Story-driven: Connect with real experiences

Closing

TA-DA Line

End with a strong line that underscores your point in a powerful way. This is your mic drop moment.

Engagement Question

Ask a simple, answerable question. Make it easy for people to engage without overthinking.

Authority ("Street Cred")

Make sure you (and your authority) show up clearly.

Your readers should feel: "I can only get this here, from YOU."

Additional Instructions

  • Speak to one person only (avoid "you all," "everyone")
  • Use "you" to pull the reader into the story
  • Address only one problem per post
  • Keep reading level around 5th grade
  • Post should take under 60 seconds to read

Spark Emotion

Every effective post triggers at least one emotion. Consider which emotions your post evokes and whether they align with your message.

Sensory Language (Optional Enhancement)

Swap dull words for sensory descriptions that create vivid mental images:

  • hurt → sting
  • bad → rough
  • unclear → murky

Overriding Principles

Every post should follow this arc:

  1. Start broad – Universal emotion or experience
  2. Narrow to niche relevance – Connect to your audience's specific situation
  3. Deepen with actionable insight – Provide value or personal resonance

Key Takeaways

Great LinkedIn content isn't about perfection—it's about connection. Use this framework to evaluate your posts before hitting publish. Ask yourself: Does this reflect my authentic voice? Does it provide clear value? Will my ideal reader stop scrolling to read it?

Start with one element from this job aid and refine from there. Your writing will improve with every post you publish.
